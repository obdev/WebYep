Bulleted Text

  • A new relief road to take through traffic around the town
  • Funding to improve bus connections to rail stations and other towns – until the number of users reaches a level where the routes can be self-funding
  • Funding to support walking and cycling around the town – including the ‘green loop’ which gives an attractive off-road alternative
  • A developer commitment to green measures in the new housing that will mean they are built to extremely high sustainability standards
  • More green space than built space, once the regeneration is complete. Whitehill & Bordon will have the third largest SANG in the country – SANG is Suitable Alternative Natural Greenspace which is land that residents can enjoy for recreation so that other habitats are protected
  • A new secondary school and a new and expanded primary school
  • Early years and post-16 education provision
  • High quality retail and leisure facilities in a new town centre which will match (or better) surrounding towns
  • A high quality six-lane swimming pool and leisure centre which will open before the existing closes
  • Significant new health and care facilities
  • Funding to support an arts and cultural centre
  • A comprehensive heritage strategy
  • A commitment to establish a community development trust to directly involve the community in the future of the town
